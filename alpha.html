Downloading the wallet

Android users, go to the Play Store and download "Status.im". iPhone users, go to the iTunes store and download "Cipher browser". The icons are shown here. Go through the steps to set up a new wallet. Common question: "Why do I need this particular wallet?"

Getting crypto

Nceno uses Ethereum's "Ether" (ETH) to transfer value instead of cash, or credit/debit cards. ETH is traded like foreign currency on thousands of online exchanges around the world. You can easily buy some from one of these exchanges, but for the Nceno Alpha, we are going to make it easier for you. Simply use Paypal, Venmo, or pay in cash, to send the amount of money you will use for your goal to: nance2uiuc or nance2uiuc@gmail.com with a message containing your wallet address, and we will send the equivalent amount of ETH to your wallet. The transaction only takes less than a minute to complete. Once you have your wallet and some ETH, go to the browser in your wallet app (looks like a compass), and type in www.Nceno.app/alpha in the address bar and click "go" (not "getting started"). You will arrive back at this page. Continue to the next step. Common questions: "How do I convert crypto into regular money again?" "How do I see my wallet address?"

Setting a goal

Note: If you followed the above steps correctly, you should now be looking at this from inside your wallet's browser, not your phone's browser. It's time to make a goal! With Nceno, you will choose a goal like: "Do 3 x 45min workouts each week for the next 4 weeks, or risk losing part of my own $50.00 USD (called your "stake"); starting from 11/01/2018". Nceno will then hold on to your cash (as ETH), giving it back bit by bit as you complete your workouts. If you don't do the number of workouts you committed to in your goal, part of your stake will be lost and sent to other people who did their workouts. Likewise, when you make progress, you will earn part of this lost stake from other people who skipped, in addition to your own stake, as reward! Click the button to set your first goal. Common questions: "Should I play it safe when setting a goal?" "How does Nceno know I did or did not workout?"
