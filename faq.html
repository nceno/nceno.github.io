Frequently asked questions

How do I convert back to "regular" money?

Since Ethereum is traded like foreign currency on thousands of exchanges around the world, it's easy to trade it out for your own native currency. In the USA, you can use Coinbase.com. In Taiwan, you can use Maicoin.com. Once your account is set up, transfers only take a day, usually. There are even companies now that issue debit cards which you can load up with crypto to spend it anywhere that accepts Visa or Mastercard.

Can I do more than my set number of workouts during each week?

Go for it! But we will only count the number of workouts you do which are less than or equal to the number you put as your goal. If you find yourself over-achieving, try setting the bar higher next time :)

Should I play it safe by setting a small number?

If you only set your stake at $5.00, do you really think the threat of losing $0.65 if you skip a workout is going to keep you going even on the days you're feeling tired? Similarly, if you set your session goal to 20min x 2 workouts/wk when you know you can easily do 60min x 3 workouts/wk, then that's not a goal. A goal should be challenging. That's why you stake money on it, so it gives you that extra kick you need to stick to it when it gets hard. In the Nceno official release, we will have group challenges where you earn money when other people fail. Do you think you're gonna get a nice payout if you set an easy goal or low stake? Probably not.

Why do I need the Trust wallet?

Nceno is built on the Ethereum Blockchain, so you need a special browser that can "sign transactions" and hold crypto. Kind of like how Ebay.com is built on the Internet, where you need a special browser that can send search requests and store your files. Blockchain is very young, compared to the internet. So while you might think it's a total pain in the butt to deal with "signing transactions" and switching out between crypto and cash, just think of how much easier to use the internet ultimately became, in less than 10 years. In the official version of Nceno, you will not need to download Trust Wallet because we will build those features internally so you won't even see them working!

Where is my wallet address?

From your wallet, you can press "receive" and you will be shown a big QR code with some numbers below starting with 0x. This is your address. It is not private, so don't worry about who you send it to, because it is not a password. It is kind of like a hard-to-read email address, but instead of messages, you are receiving ETH (money). Click it to copy it to your clipboard so you can paste it in a message.

How does Nceno verify workout progress?

In the alpha, Nceno uses a pretty flimsy way of showing evidence of working out. It's easy to cheat and it's pretty intrusive. In later versions, we will soon be using the sensors you have in your phone or your wearable to prove activity. So if you kind of like Nceno now, just wait a few months when we start to automate proof-of-activity!
